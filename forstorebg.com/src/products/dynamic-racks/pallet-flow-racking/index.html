System description

Pallet Flow racking (gravity pallet racking) is a highly efficient, high-density storage solution where pallets move automatically along gently inclined roller channels. They are loaded on one side of the rack and moved by gravity to the opposite side, where they are shipped.

The system works on the FIFO (First In, First Out) principle, which ensures that the first pallet stored is the first retrieved. This makes it particularly suitable for goods with a short shelf life or high turnover.

Thanks to the lack of internal aisles and the possibility of deep storage, this system significantly increases warehouse capacity and optimizes work processes.

Key benefits

Pallet Flow systems provide extremely efficient use of warehouse space, as they allow for the storage of a large number of pallets in depth without the need for multiple aisles. This results in a significant increase in capacity compared to standard racks.

Organizing goods using the FIFO principle improves inventory control and minimizes the risk of stockouts or expirations. This is key for industries such as food and pharmaceuticals.

The workflow is faster and more efficient as loading and unloading are done from different sides. This eliminates cross-flows and reduces forklift maneuvering time.

An additional advantage is the automatic movement of the pallets - when the first one is removed, the others move forward on their own, which speeds up the processing of goods and reduces the need for additional operations.

Areas of application

This type of racking is widely used in warehouses with high turnover of goods, where fast loading and shipping are important. It is especially suitable for logistics centers, distribution bases and manufacturing plants.

The system is ideal for storing fast-moving and time-sensitive products such as food, beverages and pharmaceuticals, as it ensures proper inventory rotation.

It is also often used as a buffer zone between production and shipping, where a continuous flow of materials and optimization of internal logistics are required.

In addition, the system finds application in cold storage facilities, where high storage density contributes to lower energy consumption.

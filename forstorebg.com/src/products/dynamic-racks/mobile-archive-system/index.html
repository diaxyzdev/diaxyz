System description

The mobile archive system is a modern solution for document storage, which allows for significant optimization of the available space. It consists of mobile racks that move on rails, thus eliminating the need for multiple fixed corridors. This makes it extremely suitable for rooms with limited space.

The main advantage of this type of system is the ability to increase storage capacity without the need to expand the room. The compact arrangement of the racks makes use of almost all of the available space, which is especially important for archives with a large volume of documentation.

Mobile archiving systems are widely used in various sectors, including administration, finance, healthcare, and cultural institutions. They provide not only efficiency, but also good organization and protection of stored information.

Key benefits

Mobile archive systems offer extremely efficient use of space, with the ability to increase storage capacity by up to twice that of standard solutions, making them ideal for organizations that deal with large volumes of documentation and have limited space.

Another important aspect is the convenience of work. The system allows quick and easy access to all archives, without having to go through multiple corridors. The movement of the racks is designed to be smooth and safe, which reduces the effort of the staff.

In addition, mobile systems offer a high level of security. They can be equipped with locking mechanisms and access control systems, ensuring the protection of important and sensitive information.

Types of systems

Mobile archive systems are available in various versions to meet the specific needs of users. The most common are manual systems, which are operated by a mechanical crank and are suitable for smaller spaces.

These systems are economical and reliable, providing basic functionality without the need for electrical power. They are the preferred choice for smaller archives or rooms with moderate traffic.

On the other hand, electrical systems offer a higher level of comfort and automation. They are controlled by buttons or intelligent panels and are suitable for large archive bases where quick and easy access to a large volume of information is required.

Comparison with static racks

Static shelving is a traditional storage solution where each row is separated by a permanent aisle. This results in a significant loss of available space, especially in larger archives.

Mobile archive systems eliminate this disadvantage by using only one working aisle. This allows for a significant increase in storage capacity without the need for additional space.

Although the initial investment for mobile systems is higher, they offer better efficiency, better organization, and long-term savings, making them the better solution in most cases.

Areas of application

Mobile archiving systems are used in a wide range of areas where large volumes of documentation need to be stored. They are particularly popular in offices and administrative buildings where space optimization is key.

In the banking and insurance sectors, these systems allow for secure storage of important documents and quick access to them when needed. This improves workflows and reduces the time spent searching for information.

Mobile archive systems are also used in libraries, museums and hospitals where the storage of documents, books or archives requires a high degree of organization and protection. They are a universal solution for any archiving-intensive environment.

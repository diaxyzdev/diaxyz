System description

The Carton Flow Rack is a dynamic warehouse system designed for the storage and picking of small to medium-sized loads. It uses inclined roller conveyors on which cartons move automatically from the loading area to the picking area under the influence of gravity.

Goods are loaded from the back of the rack and operators retrieve them from the front, and as one carton is removed, the next one automatically takes its place. This allows for a continuous flow of materials and minimizes order processing time.

The system works on the FIFO (First In, First Out) principle, which ensures proper product rotation and is particularly suitable for goods with an expiration date or high turnover.

Key benefits

Gravity box systems significantly increase the efficiency of warehouse operations. Thanks to the automatic movement of goods, operators do not have to make unnecessary movements, which leads to faster order processing and lower labor costs.

In addition, the system allows for much better use of warehouse space through high-density storage. Compared to standard racks, a significant increase in capacity and better organization of SKU items can be achieved.

Another key plus is the separation of loading and picking areas, which eliminates congestion and allows multiple operators to work simultaneously without conflict.

The system also provides excellent visibility and control over inventory, which facilitates warehouse management and reduces the risk of errors.

Areas of application

Carton Flow Rack systems are primarily used in warehouses with intensive picking operations, such as distribution centers, logistics bases and manufacturing plants. They are particularly effective in handling large numbers of small-sized, high-turnover items.

They are often used in industries such as food, pharmaceutical, electronics, and automotive components, where proper rotation of goods is critically important.

The system can be easily integrated with other warehouse solutions – pallet racks, pick-to-light systems or conveyors, making it extremely flexible when building modern logistics centers.

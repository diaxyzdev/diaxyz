System description

The compact storage system is a modern solution for storing heavy and bulky goods, where the racks are mounted on mobile platforms. They move on rails embedded in the floor, which allows only one work aisle to be opened at a time.

This type of system eliminates the need for multiple fixed aisles typical of traditional racking, thereby significantly increasing usable warehouse space. Operators have direct access to the required area without unnecessary movement of goods, making work faster and more efficient.

The system is extremely flexible and can be configured to the specific needs of the business - both for new warehouses and for the modernization of existing ones.

Key benefits

Compact storage systems provide a significant increase in storage capacity by reducing the space required to access goods. In practice, this can lead to an increase in capacity of up to 80% compared to standard solutions.

In addition, operational efficiency is improved – access to goods is faster and order processing time is reduced. This leads to higher productivity and better organization of the workflow.

The system guarantees a high level of safety, both for the stored loads and for the operators. The structure is designed to withstand intense loads and minimize the risk of damage.

A great advantage is the possibility of customization - the system can be adapted to the type of cargo, available space and specific customer requirements.

Technology (SPACEALL™ mobile bases)

The system is based on SPACEALL™ mobile bases, which use modern technologies and integrated management software. They allow precise rack movement and optimal control over warehouse operations.

The system can be implemented in both new and existing warehouses by installing rails on or in the floor. It also includes anti-seismic technology that guarantees stability and safety even under dynamic loads.

Static warehouse vs warehouse with SPACEALL™

When choosing a storage system, one of the main differences is between traditional static shelving and modern mobile solutions. Although at first glance they perform the same function, their efficiency and space utilization differ significantly.

In static warehouses, each row of racks is separated by fixed access corridors. This means that a large part of the area remains unused, as it is intended solely for the passage of equipment and operators. As a result, the actual capacity of the warehouse is limited.

On the other hand, in mobile rack warehouses, only one working aisle is used, which opens up where access is needed. All other rows are compactly packed together, allowing for significantly better utilization of the available space.

The difference is most noticeable in capacity and efficiency. While with static systems, increasing volume often requires expanding the warehouse, mobile racking allows for almost double the storage in the same area.

Areas of application

This type of warehouse solutions is particularly suitable for companies that work with heavy and bulky goods such as construction materials, industrial equipment, tires, or palletized cargo.

They are successfully used in logistics bases, manufacturing plants, distribution centers and warehouses with limited space, where area optimization is of key importance.

The system also finds application in specialized environments such as cold rooms, where it can operate at temperatures down to -35°C, without compromising on performance.

What are self-supporting warehouses?

Self-supporting warehouses are a high-tech solution in which the racking system acts as the main supporting structure of the entire building. Instead of building a separate structure of columns and beams on which racks are subsequently installed, here the racks themselves take on all the loads - both from the stored goods and from external factors such as wind, snow and seismic impacts. This makes them one of the most effective solutions in modern logistics.

This type of warehouse is distinguished by the ability to reach significant heights, often over 30–40 meters, which allows for optimal use of the available area. The lack of internal columns allows for maximum utilization of space and creates conditions for high storage density. This is especially important for companies with limited land but high capacity requirements.

Self-supporting warehouses are often combined with automated goods management systems, making them an ideal solution for modern logistics centers. Thanks to their integrated design, they allow for the construction of fully automated warehouse systems that increase efficiency and reduce the human factor.

Key benefits

One of the biggest advantages of this type of warehouse is the significant reduction in construction costs. Since no separate supporting structure is required, fewer materials are used and construction time is shortened. This results in a faster return on investment and a lower overall project budget.

Another significant advantage is the maximum use of space. Thanks to the high construction and the lack of internal partitions, a much higher capacity is achieved compared to standard warehouses. This allows the storage of large quantities of goods in a relatively small area, which is critical for industries with high turnover.

Cantilever warehouses are also highly suitable for automation. They can be easily integrated with warehouse cranes, shuttle systems and conveyor lines. This allows for fast and accurate goods management, reducing errors and significantly increasing productivity.

Types

Self-supporting warehouses can be designed to be forklift-operated, making them a more affordable solution for companies that do not require full automation. This option maintains high storage density, but the management of goods is carried out by operators.

Another main type is automated warehouses that use AS/RS systems. They include warehouse cranes and fully automated processes for storing and retrieving pallets. This solution is suitable for large logistics centers where high speed and precision are required.

There are also specialized self-supporting warehouses for refrigerated and freezer conditions. These are particularly efficient, as the compact design reduces the volume of the refrigerated space and leads to significant energy savings.

Comparison with standard warehouse buildings

The main difference between self-supporting warehouses and standard warehouses is in the construction approach. In traditional buildings, the supporting structure is built first, and then the racks are installed. In self-supporting warehouses, these two functions are combined, leading to a more efficient use of resources.

Additionally, self-supporting warehouses allow for significantly greater height and capacity, making them more suitable for companies that handle large volumes of goods and need a compact yet highly efficient solution.

In the long term, this type of warehouse offers a better return on investment, thanks to lower operating costs, automation capabilities, and higher efficiency of warehouse operations.

Areas of application

A self-supporting warehouse is an excellent choice when the available space is limited, but there is a need for maximum storage capacity. The height development of the warehouse allows to compensate for the lack of horizontal space and achieve high efficiency.

This solution is particularly suitable for companies with intensive logistics processes and a large turnover of goods. The possibility of integration with automated systems allows for significant acceleration of operations and reduction of labor costs.

Self-supporting warehouses are also preferred in the construction of modern industrial and distribution centers, where a combination of high efficiency, rapid construction, and long-term sustainability is sought.

Main components of the system

The main element of the self-supporting warehouse is the racking structure, which performs a dual function – it stores the goods and at the same time supports the entire building. It is designed to withstand high loads and ensure stability in various climatic conditions. High-strength steels and precise engineering calculations are used.

Roof and facade panels are added to the structure and are mounted directly on the racks. They provide protection from weather influences and create an enclosed storage space. Depending on the needs, different insulation materials can be used, especially in cold storage.

The foundation also plays a key role, having to take all the loads from the structure and the stored goods. In addition, various logistics technologies such as automated cranes, transport systems and management software are integrated into the system, which ensure efficient warehouse operation.

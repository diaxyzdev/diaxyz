System description

Push-Back Pallet Racking is a dynamic warehouse system for high-density pallet storage, in which loads are stacked deep on trolleys or roller tracks with a slight slope. When loading, each new pallet pushes the previous one back, and when unloading, the pallets automatically move forward by gravity.

The system operates on the LIFO (Last In, First Out) principle, with loading and picking of goods taking place from the same work lane. This eliminates the need for forklifts to enter the racks and significantly speeds up operations.

Push-back systems allow multiple pallets to be stored in a single row (typically 2 to 6 deep), with each row containing a different item. This makes them an excellent solution for warehouses with limited space and medium turnover.

Key benefits

Push-back racks provide significant optimization of warehouse space by reducing the required working aisles and increasing storage capacity. This allows better utilization of both the area and the height of the warehouse.

Working with them is extremely fast and efficient, as all operations are performed from one side. The lack of forklift entry into the structure reduces maneuvering time and increases safety during operation.

The system combines high storage density with good selectivity – each line is independent and can contain a different product without disturbing the arrangement of the others.

Push-back racks require minimal maintenance and are suitable for operation in a variety of conditions, including refrigerated and freezer warehouses, thanks to their sturdy construction and reliable mechanisms.

Areas of application

This system is particularly suitable for warehouses with medium turnover, where there are several pallets of the same item. It allows for efficient grouping of products into batches and facilitates inventory management.

Push-back racks are often used in logistics centers, production facilities and buffer zones where temporary storage of prepared orders or materials for production is required.

The system is also very suitable for cold storage facilities, as it reduces the volume of space that needs to be cooled and optimizes energy costs.

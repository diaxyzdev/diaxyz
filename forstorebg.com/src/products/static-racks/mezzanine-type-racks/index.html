System description

Mezzanine racking is a multi-level storage system that allows maximum use of the height of warehouse spaces by building raised platforms. With this construction, the available warehouse space can be doubled or tripled, without the need to expand the building or purchase new land.

The system is built from a stable steel structure consisting of supporting columns, main and secondary beams and flooring. On these structures, one or more additional levels are created, which can be used for storing goods, preparing orders or organizing workspaces.

One of the main advantages of mezzanine systems is their modularity. All elements can be dismantled and reused, and the dimensions and configuration of the structure can be easily adapted to new warehouse requirements. This allows for flexible development of the warehouse infrastructure according to business growth.

Loads to the upper levels can be transported by manual warehouse machines, lifting equipment, cranes or manually, when the weight and size of the goods allow it.

Key benefits

The mezzanine racking system offers an effective solution for increasing storage capacity by utilizing the vertical space of the building. This allows for a significant expansion of storage space without the need to construct new warehouse facilities.

The structure can be fully adapted to the specific needs of the client, thanks to the variety of possible sizes, flooring types and structural solutions. This makes it suitable for both small warehouses and large logistics centers.

Mezzanine systems are characterized by quick and easy installation, which reduces implementation time and interruptions in the work process. In addition, they can be combined with various types of warehouse racks, such as shelving or pallet systems, to create optimal warehouse organization.

Areas of application

Mezzanine systems are widely used in warehouses, logistics centers, manufacturing plants and distribution centers where maximum use of available space is required. They are particularly effective in high-ceilinged rooms where one or more additional levels can be built for storage or work.

These structures are suitable for storing a variety of goods, organizing order picking areas, as well as creating work or administrative spaces within the warehouse. Thanks to their modular structure, the systems can be easily expanded or changed as the business develops and warehouse needs increase.

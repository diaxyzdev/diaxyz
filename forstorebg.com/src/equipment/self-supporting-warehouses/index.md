<section class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceSection" id="product">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__contentTitle">
<div class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__container">
<span class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__subtitle SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__big SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__orange">What are self-supporting warehouses?</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            Self-supporting warehouses are a high-tech solution in which the
            racking system acts as the main supporting structure of the entire
            building. Instead of building a separate structure of columns and
            beams on which racks are subsequently installed, here the racks
            themselves take on all the loads - both from the stored goods and
            from external factors such as wind, snow and seismic impacts. This
            makes them one of the most effective solutions in modern logistics.
          </p>
<p>
            This type of warehouse is distinguished by the ability to reach
            significant heights, often over 30–40 meters, which allows for
            optimal use of the available area. The lack of internal columns
            allows for maximum utilization of space and creates conditions for
            high storage density. This is especially important for companies
            with limited land but high capacity requirements.
          </p>
<p>
            Self-supporting warehouses are often combined with automated goods
            management systems, making them an ideal solution for modern
            logistics centers. Thanks to their integrated design, they allow for
            the construction of fully automated warehouse systems that increase
            efficiency and reduce the human factor.
          </p>
</div>

<section class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceSection Services-module__3bADUG__reverse" id="advantages">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__contentTitle">
<div class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__container">
<span class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__subtitle SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__big SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__orange">Key benefits</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            One of the biggest advantages of this type of warehouse is the
            significant reduction in construction costs. Since no separate
            supporting structure is required, fewer materials are used and
            construction time is shortened. This results in a faster return on
            investment and a lower overall project budget.
          </p>
<p>
            Another significant advantage is the maximum use of space. Thanks to
            the high construction and the lack of internal partitions, a much
            higher capacity is achieved compared to standard warehouses. This
            allows the storage of large quantities of goods in a relatively
            small area, which is critical for industries with high turnover.
          </p>
<p>
            Cantilever warehouses are also highly suitable for automation. They
            can be easily integrated with warehouse cranes, shuttle systems and
            conveyor lines. This allows for fast and accurate goods management,
            reducing errors and significantly increasing productivity.
          </p>
</div>

<section class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceSection" id="technology">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__contentTitle">
<div class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__container">
<span class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__subtitle SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__big SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__orange">Types</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            Self-supporting warehouses can be designed to be forklift-operated,
            making them a more affordable solution for companies that do not
            require full automation. This option maintains high storage density,
            but the management of goods is carried out by operators.
          </p>
<p>
            Another main type is automated warehouses that use AS/RS systems.
            They include warehouse cranes and fully automated processes for
            storing and retrieving pallets. This solution is suitable for large
            logistics centers where high speed and precision are required.
          </p>
<p>
            There are also specialized self-supporting warehouses for
            refrigerated and freezer conditions. These are particularly
            efficient, as the compact design reduces the volume of the
            refrigerated space and leads to significant energy savings.
          </p>
</div>

<section class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceSection Services-module__3bADUG__reverse" id="comparison">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__contentTitle">
<div class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__container">
<span class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__subtitle SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__big SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__orange">Comparison with standard warehouse buildings</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            The main difference between self-supporting warehouses and standard
            warehouses is in the construction approach. In traditional
            buildings, the supporting structure is built first, and then the
            racks are installed. In self-supporting warehouses, these two
            functions are combined, leading to a more efficient use of
            resources.
          </p>
<p>
            Additionally, self-supporting warehouses allow for significantly
            greater height and capacity, making them more suitable for companies
            that handle large volumes of goods and need a compact yet highly
            efficient solution.
          </p>
<p>
            In the long term, this type of warehouse offers a better return on
            investment, thanks to lower operating costs, automation
            capabilities, and higher efficiency of warehouse operations.
          </p>
</div>

<section class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceSection" id="applications">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__contentTitle">
<div class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__container">
<span class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__subtitle SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__big SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__orange">Areas of application</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            A self-supporting warehouse is an excellent choice when the
            available space is limited, but there is a need for maximum storage
            capacity. The height development of the warehouse allows to
            compensate for the lack of horizontal space and achieve high
            efficiency.
          </p>
<p>
            This solution is particularly suitable for companies with intensive
            logistics processes and a large turnover of goods. The possibility
            of integration with automated systems allows for significant
            acceleration of operations and reduction of labor costs.
          </p>
<p>
            Self-supporting warehouses are also preferred in the construction of
            modern industrial and distribution centers, where a combination of
            high efficiency, rapid construction, and long-term sustainability is
            sought.
          </p>
</div>

<section class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceSection Services-module__3bADUG__reverse" id="components">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__contentTitle">
<div class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__container">
<span class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__subtitle SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__big SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__orange">Main components of the system</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            The main element of the self-supporting warehouse is the racking
            structure, which performs a dual function – it stores the goods and
            at the same time supports the entire building. It is designed to
            withstand high loads and ensure stability in various climatic
            conditions. High-strength steels and precise engineering
            calculations are used.
          </p>
<p>
            Roof and facade panels are added to the structure and are mounted
            directly on the racks. They provide protection from weather
            influences and create an enclosed storage space. Depending on the
            needs, different insulation materials can be used, especially in
            cold storage.
          </p>
<p>
            The foundation also plays a key role, having to take all the loads
            from the structure and the stored goods. In addition, various
            logistics technologies such as automated cranes, transport systems
            and management software are integrated into the system, which ensure
            efficient warehouse operation.
          </p>
</div>

            Mezzanine racking is a multi-level storage system that allows
            maximum use of the height of warehouse spaces by building raised
            platforms. With this construction, the available warehouse space can
            be doubled or tripled, without the need to expand the building or
            purchase new land.
          </p>
          <p>
            The system is built from a stable steel structure consisting of
            supporting columns, main and secondary beams and flooring. On these
            structures, one or more additional levels are created, which can be
            used for storing goods, preparing orders or organizing workspaces.
          </p>
          <p>
            One of the main advantages of mezzanine systems is their modularity.
            All elements can be dismantled and reused, and the dimensions and
            configuration of the structure can be easily adapted to new
            warehouse requirements. This allows for flexible development of the
            warehouse infrastructure according to business growth.
          </p>
          <p>
            Loads to the upper levels can be transported by manual warehouse
            machines, lifting equipment, cranes or manually, when the weight and
            size of the goods allow it.
          </p>

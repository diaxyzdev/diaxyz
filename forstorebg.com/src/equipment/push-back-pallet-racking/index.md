<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            Push-Back Pallet Racking is a dynamic warehouse system for
            high-density pallet storage, in which loads are stacked deep on
            trolleys or roller tracks with a slight slope. When loading, each
            new pallet pushes the previous one back, and when unloading, the
            pallets automatically move forward by gravity.
          </p>
<p>
            The system operates on the LIFO (Last In, First Out) principle, with
            loading and picking of goods taking place from the same work lane.
            This eliminates the need for forklifts to enter the racks and
            significantly speeds up operations.
          </p>
<p>
            Push-back systems allow multiple pallets to be stored in a single
            row (typically 2 to 6 deep), with each row containing a different
            item. This makes them an excellent solution for warehouses with
            limited space and medium turnover.
          </p>
</div>

<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
<div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
<p>
            Push-back racks provide significant optimization of warehouse space
            by reducing the required working aisles and increasing storage
            capacity. This allows better utilization of both the area and the
            height of the warehouse.
          </p>
<p>
            Working with them is extremely fast and efficient, as all operations
            are performed from one side. The lack of forklift entry into the
            structure reduces maneuvering time and increases safety during
            operation.
          </p>
<p>
            The system combines high storage density with good selectivity –
            each line is independent and can contain a different product without
            disturbing the arrangement of the others.
          </p>
<p>
            Push-back racks require minimal maintenance and are suitable for
            operation in a variety of conditions, including refrigerated and
            freezer warehouses, thanks to their sturdy construction and reliable
            mechanisms.
          </p>
</div>

            turnover, where there are several pallets of the same item. It

            facilitates inventory management.

            Push-back racks are often used in logistics centers, production

            orders or materials for production is required.

            reduces the volume of space that needs to be cooled and optimizes
            energy costs.

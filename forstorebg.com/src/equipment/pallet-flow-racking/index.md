          <p>
            Pallet Flow racking (gravity pallet racking) is a highly efficient,
            high-density storage solution where pallets move automatically along
            gently inclined roller channels. They are loaded on one side of the
            rack and moved by gravity to the opposite side, where they are
            shipped.
          </p>
          <p>
            The system works on the FIFO (First In, First Out) principle, which
            ensures that the first pallet stored is the first retrieved. This
            makes it particularly suitable for goods with a short shelf life or
            high turnover.
          </p>
          <p>
            Thanks to the lack of internal aisles and the possibility of deep
            storage, this system significantly increases warehouse capacity and
            optimizes work processes.
          </p>

          <p>
            Pallet Flow systems provide extremely efficient use of warehouse
            space, as they allow for the storage of a large number of pallets in
            depth without the need for multiple aisles. This results in a
            significant increase in capacity compared to standard racks.
          </p>
          <p>
            Organizing goods using the FIFO principle improves inventory control
            and minimizes the risk of stockouts or expirations. This is key for
            industries such as food and pharmaceuticals.
          </p>
          <p>
            The workflow is faster and more efficient as loading and unloading
            are done from different sides. This eliminates cross-flows and
            reduces forklift maneuvering time.
          </p>
          <p>
            An additional advantage is the automatic movement of the pallets -
            when the first one is removed, the others move forward on their own,
            which speeds up the processing of goods and reduces the need for
            additional operations.
          </p>

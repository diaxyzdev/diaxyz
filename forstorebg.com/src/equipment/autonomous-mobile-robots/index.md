            AMRs (autonomous mobile robots) are intelligent transport devices
            that move independently within a warehouse or production space,
            without the need for pre-built routes. They use virtual maps,
            sensors and software to determine the most efficient path between
            different points.
          </p>

            obstacles. This makes them ideal for dynamic warehouses where
            conditions and flows frequently change.
          </p>
          <p>

            transportation of pallets, cartons, and various cargo, reducing the
            need for manual labor and increasing the efficiency of operations.

  <section class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceSection" id="technology">
    <div class="Services-module__3bADUG__contentTitle">
      <div class="SectionTitle-module__Svbyca__container">
        <span

          >How autonomous mobile robots work</span
        >
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="Services-module__3bADUG__container">
      <div class="Services-module__3bADUG__serviceGrid">
        <div class="Services-module__3bADUG__textBlock">
          <p>
            AMR begins by creating a digital map of the warehouse by scanning
            the space. This map is used by all robots in the system to navigate
            and perform tasks.
          </p>
          <p>
            Each robot receives tasks from a central management software (fleet
            management system), which optimizes the movement of all devices and
            distributes tasks based on load and distances. This allows for
            maximum efficiency and avoidance of traffic jams.
          </p>
          <p>
            Using technologies such as LiDAR sensors, cameras and ultrasonic
            sensors, AMR robots can detect people and objects and change their
            route in real time, ensuring safe operation in a shared environment.
          </p>
        </div>

            The main difference between AMR and AGV (Automated Guided Vehicles)
            is the way they navigate. AGVs follow pre-defined routes, while AMRs
            move freely and choose the optimal path in real time.
          </p>
          <p>
            This makes AMRs significantly more flexible and suitable for dynamic
            environments where warehouse layouts can change. AGV systems are
            better suited for stable and repeatable processes.
          </p>
          <p>
            Overall, AMRs are considered the next evolution in warehouse
            automation, delivering higher efficiency, adaptability, and lower
            long-term operating costs.

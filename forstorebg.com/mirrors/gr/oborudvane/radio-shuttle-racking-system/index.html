Radio shuttle rack systemRadio Shuttle Racking System

An intelligent solution for high-density pallet storage with maximum efficiency and minimum operator intervention

DescriptionAdvantagesApplicationsComponentsGallery
System description

The radio shuttle system is a modern solution for automated pallet storage that uses an electric shuttle (shuttle) moving inside the channels of the racking system. It takes over the tasks of getting the pallets in and out, eliminating the need for forklifts to enter the racks.

The system combines high stocking density with process automation, resulting in a significant increase in productivity and optimal use of available space.

The radio shuttle can work both in semi-automatic mode (with forklifts) and in fully automated systems with transfer trolleys or stacker cranes, making it an extremely flexible solution for various warehouse needs.

Key benefits

Radio shuttle systems significantly improve warehouse efficiency through automation and space optimization. They allow for much greater storage capacity thanks to deep storage channels and a reduction in work aisles.

In addition, the system increases the processing speed of the pallets, as the shuttle moves quickly inside the channels and reduces the time for loading and unloading operations.

Another key plus is safety – as the forklifts do not enter the racks, the risk of accidents and damage is significantly lower.

The radio shuttle system also enables, continuous operation with high productivity, reduction of human errors, operation in different temperature conditions, including cold storage, management and control through a WMS system

Areas of application

The radio shuttle system is extremely suitable for warehouses with large volumes and high turnover intensity.

It is widely used in logistics centers, manufacturing plants and distribution warehouses where large quantities of pallets of the same item are stored

The system is particularly effective in fast moving goods, shipping buffer zones, food processing and cold storage, manufacturing and intermediate storage.

Thanks to its high density, it is an excellent solution for limited storage space where maximum utilization of volume is critical.

The process starts with feeding the pallet to the entry area via a conveyor or forklift. The automated system or operator then positions the shuttle in the appropriate channel.

The shuttle picks up the pallet, lifts it slightly and transports it to the deepest free position in the channel, where it is placed on the rails. When subtracting, the process is done in reverse order.

In fully automated systems, shuttle movement is coordinated by software (WMS/WCS) that manages all warehouse operations and ensures optimal flow of goods.
